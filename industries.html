Built for firms where data sensitivity is non-negotiable.

We work with four industries deeply because that's where private AI delivers the highest measurable ROI — and where public AI carries the most career-ending risk.

For Law Firms

Boutique & mid-size law firms

"Your client files never touch OpenAI's servers."

10–50 attorney practices in litigation, transactional, family, estate planning and IP — where attorney-client privilege isn't theoretical and the bar's ethics opinions on cloud AI keep getting stricter.

Specific pain points

  • Associates and paralegals using ChatGPT on the side, exposing privileged work product the firm doesn't even know about
  • Document review and discovery costs that haven't dropped despite a decade of "AI-powered" eDiscovery vendors
  • Billing narratives written from scratch by attorneys at $400/hour because the BigLaw-grade tools don't exist for boutique-size budgets
  • Intake conflicts checks, matter summaries and client status updates eating partner time that should be billable

Specific AI use cases

  • Private intake triage that classifies new matters and routes them with a draft engagement memo attached
  • Document review and summarization on a self-hosted model — discovery, deposition prep, contract markup
  • Billing narrative drafting from time entries, in your firm's voice and your client's preferred format
  • Matter status auto-summaries pushed to clients on a cadence — drafted by AI, reviewed in 30 seconds by an attorney
  • Knowledge base over your own firm's prior work product — never indexed by a third party

Example ROI — a 22-attorney litigation firm saves 11 hours per attorney per week on billing, intake and matter summaries. At a conservative blended rate, that's roughly $650K of recovered capacity per year on a sub-$50K all-in investment.

For Med Spas

Med spas & aesthetics practices

"From front desk to follow-up — automated."

Single and multi-location med spas where every missed follow-up is a $1,200 package walking out the door — and where front-desk turnover destroys consistent customer experience.

Specific pain points

  • Inbound leads from Instagram, Google and referrals dying in the inbox over weekends and after hours
  • No systematic recall for six-week and twelve-week treatment cycles — clients drift to competitors who message them first
  • Front-desk staff turnover constantly resetting customer experience to baseline mediocrity
  • Reviews not being requested at the right moment, killing local SEO and paid-ad efficiency

Specific AI use cases

  • Lead-response automation that qualifies, books and confirms within 90 seconds of inquiry — 24/7
  • Treatment-aware recall sequences that know when each client is due for tox, filler, lasers or memberships
  • Personalized post-treatment follow-up texts that feel hand-written because they're trained on your tone
  • Review requests timed to the moment of highest satisfaction, with intelligent escalation if a client signals dissatisfaction
  • Operations dashboard answering "what's our actual revenue per chair, per provider, per service?" in plain English

Example ROI — a two-location med spa adds ~$18K/month in recovered revenue from faster lead response and systematic recall, plus 15 hours/week of front-desk capacity returned to in-room support.

For Healthcare

Healthcare & behavioral health

"HIPAA-adjacent AI that actually helps your practice."

Behavioral health, primary care and specialty practices where the conversation about AI usually stops at "we can't because HIPAA." We start the conversation at "what does HIPAA actually require, and how do we build for it?"

Specific pain points

  • Provider burnout from documentation burden — clinicians spending more time charting than with patients
  • Intake forms transcribed manually into the EHR, with errors that surface as billing problems three months later
  • Prior auth, referrals and external records requests done by hand because no off-the-shelf tool can be HIPAA-compliant inside a small practice
  • Patient outreach for screenings, refills and behavioral health check-ins falling off entirely when staff is short

Specific AI use cases

  • Private model with signed BAA, deployed in your environment, processing PHI without ever sending it externally
  • Pre-visit chart prep summaries built from the last 18 months of encounters, ready in the provider's inbox each morning
  • Intake form parsing into structured EHR data with automated flagging of red-flag responses
  • Recall outreach and screening reminders, personalized and clinically appropriate
  • Audit logging on every prompt and response, retained per your compliance policy

Example ROI — a 6-provider behavioral health group reclaims ~90 minutes per provider per day from documentation and prep, equivalent to 2–3 additional billable visits per provider per week.

For Financial Services

RIAs, CPAs & wealth managers

"Compliance-ready AI for regulated workflows."

Independent RIAs, small CPA firms and boutique wealth managers operating under SEC, FINRA or state oversight — where every client communication is potentially examinable and every document needs to be retainable.

Specific pain points

  • Compliance teams blanket-banning AI tools because nobody can promise the audit trail will hold up in an exam
  • Client meeting prep eating 60–90 minutes per meeting that should take 15
  • Quarterly client letters and portfolio reviews written from templates that nobody updates
  • CPA firms drowning in the same client document chase every January through April

Specific AI use cases

  • Pre-meeting client briefs synthesizing CRM notes, portfolio changes, market context and prior commitments
  • Compliance-aware drafting that flags potentially problematic language before it leaves the firm
  • Quarterly client letter generation personalized to each client's portfolio and prior conversations
  • Document collection workflows that chase clients politely and persistently without staff hand-holding
  • Books-and-records-grade audit logging on every AI-assisted communication

Example ROI — an 8-advisor RIA cuts client-meeting prep from 75 to 15 minutes per meeting, recovering roughly 20 hours per advisor per month for actual client-facing time and new business.
