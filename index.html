The Problem

Public AI is a liability your firm cannot ignore.

Every time a paralegal pastes a contract into ChatGPT, or a nurse uploads intake notes into Copilot, your sensitive data enters a public model's training pipeline.

One

Your confidential data is training someone else's model.

ChatGPT and Copilot are not built for privileged communications. Under ABA Rule 1.6, uploading client data to these tools may constitute unauthorized disclosure — and most firms have no policy in place.

Two

Off-the-shelf AI doesn't know your business.

A model trained on the internet knows nothing about your matter types, billing codes, intake forms, or compliance requirements. Generic answers from a generic model aren't useful — they're dangerous.

Three

Your competitors built private AI last year.

Larger firms have already deployed internal AI handling intake, document drafting, and billing prep. The boutique market is two years behind — and the gap is widening every quarter.

The Solution

Private AI built around your business. Not the internet.

We deploy open-weights language models on your infrastructure, fine-tune them on your data, and wrap them in the workflow automations your team actually uses — without a single byte leaving your environment.
