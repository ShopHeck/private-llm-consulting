FAQ

Common questions about private LLMs and data security.

What does "private LLM" actually mean? +
A private LLM is a large language model — typically an open-weights model like Meta's Llama or Mistral's family — that runs entirely on infrastructure you control. That can be a server in your office, a private cloud tenant (your own AWS/Azure/GCP account), or a dedicated GPU instance with a hosting partner you contract with directly. The critical distinction: at no point does your prompt, document, or response leave your environment to be seen, logged, or used for training by a third party.
How is this different from just using ChatGPT Enterprise or Claude for Teams? +
Enterprise versions of public LLMs offer better contractual guarantees than the consumer products — your data isn't used for training, and there are SOC 2 reports — but the data still travels to and is processed on OpenAI or Anthropic infrastructure. For many firms that's an acceptable trade-off. For regulated industries, professional-services firms with strict client confidentiality obligations, or anyone who has been burned by a vendor breach, "trust us" isn't sufficient. Private deployment makes the question moot.
Are private models actually as good as GPT-4 or Claude? +
For general-purpose chat, the frontier closed models still have an edge. For domain-specific tasks — drafting an intake summary using your firm's exact format, classifying a patient inquiry against your triage rules, generating a billing narrative in your voice — a fine-tuned open-weights model often matches or beats them, because it's trained on the work that actually matters to you. We benchmark every deployment against the closed alternatives and only ship if it meets or exceeds them on your specific workflows.
What infrastructure do I actually need to host a private LLM? +
Less than most people assume. A small to mid-sized firm typically needs either (a) a single GPU server — capital cost roughly $8K–$25K depending on model size — or (b) a dedicated GPU instance from a private hosting provider for $400–$2,000/month. The audit identifies which option fits your usage profile. We handle sizing, procurement guidance and deployment as part of Tier 3.
Do you sign a BAA / handle HIPAA, attorney-client privilege, or other compliance requirements? +
Yes — and the architecture is built for it. For healthcare clients we sign a Business Associate Agreement and design every workflow around HIPAA's minimum-necessary and audit-logging requirements. For law firms, the entire reason private deployment exists is to keep privileged communications inside the firm's wall. For financial services, we build for the recordkeeping and supervision requirements relevant to your registration. We're not a compliance attorney, but we build infrastructure that makes your compliance attorney's job substantially easier.
